# Activity 1 — What You'll Walk Away With

**Phase:** 2 — Ideation Sprint  
**Type:** Linear (run once, at phase entry)  
**Estimated time:** 10 minutes  
**Prerequisite:** Phase 1 Problem Proof Pack submitted with Proceed or Pivot decision  
**Output:** Orientation only. No graded artifact.

---

## Purpose

Set the promise. Frame Phase 2 as a loop, not a checklist.

Most teams arrive here with one of two wrong mental models:

1. "I have a problem. Now I brainstorm solutions and pick the best one."
2. "I need to build something. Let me start designing."

Both of those mental models skip the most important step: **testing whether any solution actually works before building it.**

Activity 1 corrects both mental models before they take hold.

---

## What Participants Should Understand After This Activity

- Phase 2 is not about finding the right idea. It is about finding out which idea survives contact with real users.
- The phase has a loop. Prototype, test, learn, decide. Repeat if needed.
- Running the loop twice is not failure. It is the method.
- The end state is not a working product. It is a tested idea with enough evidence to decide whether to build it.

---

## Content Brief

### Format

Short webtoon / visual intro. Not a wall of text.

### What the webtoon should show

1. A team with a Phase 1 Problem Proof Pack in hand — they know the problem. They don't know the solution yet.
2. The team brainstorming lots of ideas — showing that one idea gets selected, not the first one that comes up.
3. A fast, rough prototype being built — paper, not code.
4. The prototype in front of a real user — who reacts in a way the team did not expect.
5. The team synthesizing what they learned and deciding: loop again, or move to build.
6. A clean end state: Prototype Evidence Pack in hand, ready for Phase 3.

### Tone

- Concrete, not inspirational
- Honest that the first prototype will probably reveal something unexpected
- Reassuring that the loop is the point, not a penalty

### Key visual element

Show the loop diagram explicitly. Teams should see the loop before they start. Not a surprise.

```
Generate ideas
      ↓
Select best bet + hypothesis
      ↓
   ┌──────────────────┐
   │ Choose method    │
   │ Build prototype  │  ← loop (1–3x)
   │ Test with users  │
   │ Synthesize       │
   └──────┬───────────┘
          │ Proceed
          ↓
   Submit Evidence Pack
```

---

## Re-entry Note for Pivot Teams

Teams who Pivoted in Phase 1 enter here with a revised problem framing — not a blank slate.

This activity should surface a brief message for Pivot teams:

> You Pivoted in Phase 1. That means you have a revised problem framing but the same evidence base. Your job in Phase 2 is to test solution ideas against your revised framing. You don't start from zero — you start with sharper constraints.

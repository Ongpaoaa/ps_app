# Activity 2 — Generate Many Ideas, Judge None Yet

**Phase:** 2 — Ideation Sprint  
**Type:** Linear (run once)  
**Estimated time:** 30 minutes  
**Prerequisite:** Activity 1 complete  
**Output:** Idea shortlist — minimum 5 distinct ideas

---

## Purpose

Break teams out of first-idea anchoring.

The single most common failure mode in hackathon ideation is selecting the first decent idea and running with it. Teams do this because:

- generating ideas takes creative energy and teams are already tired
- the first idea feels "good enough"
- evaluating ideas feels risky — what if none of them are good?

This activity creates a structure that forces idea volume before evaluation begins. You cannot evaluate well without options.

---

## Learning Objective

By the end of this activity, a team should have:

- 5 or more genuinely distinct solution ideas (not variations of one concept)
- each idea connected (even loosely) to the Phase 1 leverage point
- no evaluation, ranking, or rejection done yet

---

## Instructions for Participants

### Step 1 — Bring your Phase 1 leverage point into the room

Before generating ideas, re-read your Phase 1 Problem Proof Pack together. Specifically:

- What is the leverage point you identified?
- Who is the target user?
- What behavior keeps the problem alive?

Write these three things on a shared surface before you start generating.

This is not optional. Ideas that are disconnected from Phase 1 evidence waste time. You are not here to brainstorm anything. You are here to brainstorm **responses to a real system you already understand.**

### Step 2 — Set a timer for 20 minutes. Generate ideas. No judgment.

Rules during generation:

- Write down every idea, including the ones that feel dumb
- No criticism, no "we can't do that", no "that already exists"
- Every team member generates independently for the first 10 minutes, then share
- Quantity is the goal — aim for at least 10 ideas as a team before selecting anything

### Step 3 — Check for real variety

Before you move on, look at your list. Ask:

- Are these 5 variations of the same idea, or are they genuinely different?
- Do any of them address the leverage point from Phase 1 in a fundamentally different way?
- Is there at least one idea that feels uncomfortable or risky?

If all your ideas are variations of the same concept, set another 5-minute timer and force 3 ideas that approach the problem from a different direction.

### Step 4 — Submit your shortlist

Write a one-line description for each idea. Submit the list.

You are not committing to any of these ideas yet. You are building the option set for Activity 3.

---

## Idea Diversity Checklist

Teams should be able to answer yes to at least 3 of these before submitting:

- [ ] Our list contains ideas that differ in the **type of solution** (product vs service vs information vs community)
- [ ] Our list contains at least one idea that we are uncertain we can build
- [ ] Our list contains at least one idea that surprised us
- [ ] Our list contains ideas that address the leverage point in different ways
- [ ] No single idea appears more than once in different wordings

---

## Common Failure Modes

**Failure mode 1: The variations trap**

Team generates 10 ideas that are all the same app with slightly different features. Address this by asking: "If a user had a totally different relationship with this problem — a vendor instead of a customer, a teacher instead of a student — what would they want?"

**Failure mode 2: The evaluation creep**

Team starts arguing about why an idea won't work while still in generation mode. Stop it immediately. Judgment comes in Activity 3. Right now the job is volume.

**Failure mode 3: The Phase 1 disconnect**

Team generates ideas that are interesting but have nothing to do with the system they mapped in Phase 1. These ideas will fail testing because they are not addressing the real problem. Reconnect to the leverage point.

**Failure mode 4: The solo anchor**

One team member dominates ideation and the rest nod along. Enforce independent generation in the first 10 minutes. Everyone writes their own list before sharing.

## AI Feedback

AI feedback does not fire on this activity by default. Optional AI trigger: if a team submits a list where all ideas have very similar descriptions, the AI flags it:

> "Your ideas look similar. Before moving to selection, try pushing one idea in a completely different direction — not a different feature, but a different model. What if the solution had no app at all? What if it worked through a person instead of a product?"

---

## Mentor Guidance

Mentors can review the shortlist and comment. Non-blocking.

Look for:

- Real variety (not 5 versions of the same product)
- Connection to Phase 1 evidence
- At least one idea that feels risky or unconventional

If the list looks homogeneous, flag it: "I see variations of one idea here. Can you push one idea in a totally different direction before selecting?"

---

## Grading

### Completion check (pass/fail)

| Requirement | Pass condition |
|---|---|
| Idea count | 5 or more ideas submitted |
| Descriptions | Each idea has a one-line description |

### Rubric dimension: Idea diversity

| Level | Criteria |
|---|---|
| 0 | Fewer than 5 ideas, or no submission |
| 1 | 5+ ideas but they are variations of the same concept |
| 2 | 5+ ideas with genuine variety in approach or model |
| 3 | 5+ ideas with variety, at least one unexpected or unconventional idea, and visible connection to the Phase 1 leverage point across the list |

This dimension feeds into the phase-level Evidence Pack rubric (Dimension 1: Idea grounding).

```
Idea 1: [one-line description]
Idea 2: [one-line description]
Idea 3: [one-line description]
Idea 4: [one-line description]
Idea 5: [one-line description]
[Idea 6–N: optional]
```

**What the app stores:**

- list of idea strings, indexed by position
- team ID
- timestamp
